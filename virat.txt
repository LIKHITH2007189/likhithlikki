VIRAT KOHLI is an iconic Indian cricketer, considered one of the best batsmen of his generation, known for his aggressive style, incredible fitness, and leadership as the former captain across all formats for India. Nicknamed "King Kohli," he's a prolific run-scorer, particularly dominant in One Day Internationals (ODIs), holding numerous records, including most centuries, and is famous for his ability to "chase down" targets. He captains Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) in the IPL and is married to actress Anushka Sharma, forming a major celebrity couple in India. 
Key Highlights:
Batting Prowess: A top-order, right-handed batsman known for his technique and powerful stroke play.
"Chase Master": Excels at leading India to victory in limited-overs chases.
Leadership: A highly successful captain for India, leading them to significant Test series wins, including in Australia.
Fitness Icon: Sets high standards for fitness and discipline in world cricket.
Records: Holds records for most ODI centuries, leading run-scorer in IPL, and is a recipient of major ICC awards.
IPL Career: Plays for Royal Challengers Bengaluru in the Indian Premier League.
Personal Life: Married to Bollywood star Anushka Sharma; they have two children. 
Early Life & Rise:
Born in Delhi on November 5, 1988, Kohli showed early promise, leading India to the U-19 World Cup victory in 2008 before making his senior international debut that same year. 
Impact:
Kohli's dedication, work ethic, and inspiring performances have made him a global icon, a role model for youth, and a massive social media presence, cementing his place among cricket's all-time greats.
